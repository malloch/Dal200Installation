MIT

She grew up in the Midwest Indiana Ohio Michigan Upper Penninsula, USA. In 1958, she received her undergraduate degree in mathematics and worked as a programmer at MIT.

NASA

She was the first programmer hired by NASA and worked on MIT’s Apollo project: landing a man on the moon.

HUMAN ERROR

She added code to the Apollo flight Software to account for human error. This code was the largest contribution to the Apollo 11 mission in 1969 and stopped a system overload when a crew member set a switch in the wrong position. The software could handled the error, and the astronauts successfully landed on the moon.

AWARDS

She was awarded the NASA Exceptional Space Act Award for her scientific and technical contributions. She was chosen as a recipient of the Presidential Medal of Freedom by U.S. President Barack Obama.

Landing on the Moon
Margaret Hamilton
