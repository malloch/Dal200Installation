PHD IN MATHEMATICS

Grace studied math and physics at Vassar College and Yale University. she earned a Ph.D. in mathematics in 1934 and was the fist few women earn this degree.

WORLD WAR II

During World War II, she was compelled to join the U.S. Naval Reserve and assigned to the Bureau of Ordnance Computation Project at Harvard University, where she learned to program a Mark I computer.

CAREER IN COMPUTING

After World War II, she worked with the Mark II and Mark III computers at Harvard. In 1952, her team created the first compiler for computer languages.
